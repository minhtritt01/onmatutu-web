Midnight on a Friday. Hà just finished her shift at the bar, gets back to her rented room, showers quickly, opens her notebook. There's a test tomorrow. Her hair isn't even dry yet.

![Moment 1](/characters/06-07-2026-sinh-vien-1.png)

The coffee on her desk went cold without her noticing. She doesn't complain to anyone — not because it isn't hard, but because explaining it is its own kind of tiring. Right now her classmates are asleep or watching something. The coffee's cold. She keeps studying a little longer.

![Moment 2](/characters/06-07-2026-sinh-vien-2.png)

Thursday morning, her head drops onto the desk mid-class. A classmate asks why she's so tired — she just laughs it off. No one in her class knows she waits tables three nights a week. They only see the dozing, and she doesn't explain any further.

![Moment 3](/characters/06-07-2026-sinh-vien-3.png)

Not every night is like this. But on the nights that are, she always pushes through one more chapter before sleep. She closes the book, turns off the lamp — no one's here to tell her she did well, but she knows it herself.

> No one pats her shoulder to say well done.
> She knows herself that she tried.

Some effort goes unseen by everyone but you — are you quietly pushing through something like this too?
