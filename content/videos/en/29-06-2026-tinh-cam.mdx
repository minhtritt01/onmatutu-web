Two years. They sit next to each other every class, walk out together, laugh about assignments. Everything looks ordinary — except his heart.

![Moment 1](/characters/29-06-2026-tinh-cam-1.png)

At night, Khánh opens Zalo and types the name into search. The profile appears. His hand stops. It's not that he doesn't know what to say — his thumb just won't press send. This is the dozenth time. He closes the app. Still remembers every character in that name.

![Moment 2](/characters/29-06-2026-tinh-cam-2.png)

After class they walk out together toward the elevator. His heart is racing, but all he talks about is homework. At the intersection they separate — they turn left. He stands there one extra second, not sure why. Just not ready to walk away yet.

![Moment 3](/characters/29-06-2026-tinh-cam-3.png)

One afternoon his classmate leaves before him. He scolds himself: *just say it, how hard can it be.* But it is hard. Not because he's afraid of being turned down — but because if he says something and gets a no, the *maybe* disappears. So he stays quiet. One more day.

> It's not the rejection he's afraid of.
> It's losing the maybe.

How many people are holding their silence not out of cowardice, but because the possibility feels more precious than certainty?
