11:30pm. The house is dark. Binh walks into the kitchen — there's a plate of rice on the table, covered by a bowl flipped upside down. Sour soup, braised fish. Still warm, because Dad had put it in the rice cooker. No note. No text. Just the plate.

![Moment 1](/characters/30-06-2026-chung-1.png)

He worked overtime until 11:30. The whole house was asleep. In the kitchen — a covered plate, soup and fish still warm. His dad hadn't written anything, hadn't texted. Just the food sitting quietly in the dark, waiting.

![Moment 2](/characters/30-06-2026-chung-2.png)

He sat alone eating in the dark kitchen. The rice was warm — and that's when it hit him: his dad hadn't gone to sleep yet. He'd waited up, set out the food, and only then gone to bed. Not a single word. Just warm rice in the middle of the night.

![Moment 3](/characters/30-06-2026-chung-3.png)

His dad criticized often, rarely praised. But every late night he came home, the kitchen smelled of rice. That was how his dad said he loved him — not in words, but in a covered plate and a rice cooker kept warm.

> Dad never said "I love you."
> The smell of late-night rice said it instead.
