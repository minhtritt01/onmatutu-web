The 5th. Payday. He taps through three transfers — rent, money for his mom, phone installment. Three taps. 1.8 million left. 25 days to go. He does the math: 72,000 dong a day, covering food, gas, and everything else. This city isn't cheap. And he doesn't spend carelessly.

![Moment 1](/characters/28-06-2026-van-phong-1.png)

Payday means paying. Rent, his mom, the phone installment — all gone in one morning. 1.8 million left. He keeps running the number and it keeps coming out the same: 72k a day. The math is right. It just doesn't feel livable.

![Moment 2](/characters/28-06-2026-van-phong-2.png)

A friend texts — birthday dinner this weekend. He types "I'm busy" and puts the phone down. His fingers finish the sentence. His chest aches a little. Not because he doesn't want to go. Because he doesn't have 200k to sit at that table.

![Moment 3](/characters/28-06-2026-van-phong-3.png)

That evening, he opens the banking app for the fourth time. His eyes are tired. The number hasn't changed — he already knew it wouldn't. He puts the phone down. Goes to bed early so he won't be drowsy at work tomorrow. That's the only thing he can control right now.

> The salary isn't enough to live on in this city.
> But it's enough to send something home to his mom.

Khoa isn't reckless with money. He isn't giving up. The salary just hasn't caught up to the cost of living here. Do you know that feeling — when you've done all the math right, and it still comes up short?
