Every night, Quang opens a delivery app instead of cooking. Not because he's lazy — standing in the kitchen to cook always brings back his mother's kitchen back home.

![Moment 1](/characters/09-07-2026-chung-1.png)

Cooking means remembering. So he just taps to order, and leaves the kitchen light off, unused — a habit he repeated more times than he could count, just to avoid facing that feeling.

![Moment 2](/characters/09-07-2026-chung-2.png)

But this Thursday night, he cooked. Turned on the TV, sat down to eat. First bite in, he paused — the dish tasted exactly like what his mother used to make. He didn't say anything. Just kept eating, no rush.

![Moment 3](/characters/09-07-2026-chung-3.png)

When he finished, he felt a little homesick. Not sad, really — just a little. Then he stood up and kept tidying up, because tomorrow still had things to do, and life was still waiting.

> Missing home doesn't make me weaker.
> It's just one more reason to try.

Some kinds of missing don't need to be hidden — they just need to become a reason to keep going. What are you homesick for, and is it pushing you to try a little harder?
