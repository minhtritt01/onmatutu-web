4pm, class just let out. Tâm doesn't head back to his room to rest — he runs straight to the café, just in time for his shift. Stomach empty, textbooks still stuffed in his backpack. But the shift doesn't wait for anyone.

![Moment 1](/characters/08-07-2026-sinh-vien-1.png)

Before he can eat a single bite, he's already behind the counter. Tying the apron, he doesn't think too hard about it — he just works, because this night shift is the only thing standing between him and this term's tuition.

![Moment 2](/characters/08-07-2026-sinh-vien-2.png)

11pm, wiping down tables on the last shift, his legs sore from seven hours on his feet. He counts the tips: 45,000 đồng. Add the wage, add what his parents sent — just enough. He tucks the money into a cloth pouch, no celebration, just a long breath out.

![Moment 3](/characters/08-07-2026-sinh-vien-3.png)

The next morning in class, his eyes are still shadowed. A classmate asks why he looks so tired — he just says he studied late. No one knows about the café. He doesn't plan to tell them either. He just lets it be, and keeps studying.

> No one counts the hours I stood.
> This term's tuition — paid by my own hand.

Some effort goes unseen, and doesn't need to be seen to count. Are you quietly carrying something you've never told anyone about?
