His hand was shaking. The results board came up — 3 points short of passing. Dung stood there for a while, then quietly turned away. Back home, whenever anyone asked, he just said, "haven't taken it yet this term."

![Moment 1](/characters/01-07-2026-sinh-vien-1.png)

He studied for three more months. Told no one he'd failed — just closed his door and opened the books again from scratch. Nobody knew he'd already lost once.

![Moment 2](/characters/01-07-2026-sinh-vien-2.png)

Second attempt — failed again, this time by just 1 point. On the bus home, Dung didn't cry, just looked out the window. Everyone around him went about their day like nothing happened; none of them knew he'd just failed a second time. He thought about his parents, about the exam fees, and stayed quiet.

![Moment 3](/characters/01-07-2026-sinh-vien-3.png)

The bus stopped at a red light. Dung opened his phone, went to the registration page, and signed up for his third attempt right then — still on the bus. Not because he wasn't sad. He knew that if he waited until he got home, he wouldn't have the courage left.

> Afraid that going home would break his resolve.
> Register first, be sad about it later.

If it were you — would you register first, or wait until the sadness passed?
