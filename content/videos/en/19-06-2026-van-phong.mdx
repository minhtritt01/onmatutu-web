A few days after quitting. Barely had time to breathe when an old friend texted — sent a screenshot. Hands trembling opening it.

![Moment 1](/characters/19-06-2026-van-phong-1.png)

Not good news. A group chat — people talking about him. Not angry. Just found it kind of funny. He'd barely left and they already had things to say.

![Moment 2](/characters/19-06-2026-van-phong-2.png)

A few days later, ran into someone from the old office on the street. They avoided eye contact. The one who always smiled at him the most. No explanation needed. Found out last. That's just how it is.

![Moment 3](/characters/19-06-2026-van-phong-3.png)

Knew about it. Started typing a reply. Typed, deleted. Typed again, deleted. Left it. They'd already made up their minds — nothing left to say. Choosing silence isn't the same as having nothing to say. It just means it's not worth saying anymore.

> Say whatever you want.
> They've already decided.

Some people you thought were colleagues turn out to be just people who sat in the same room. Have you ever felt this way?
