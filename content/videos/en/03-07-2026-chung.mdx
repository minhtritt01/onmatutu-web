9am. Linh opens her laptop — a client project is still waiting. She stares at the blank screen for a while, then closes it. Not laziness — the words just aren't there today.

![Moment 1](/characters/03-07-2026-chung-1.png)

She opens the laptop at 9am and stares at a blank screen for twenty minutes. Not a single line comes out. She used to force herself to sit there until something appeared, even just one sentence. Today she closes the laptop instead, and stops trying.

![Moment 2](/characters/03-07-2026-chung-2.png)

By 11am the project is still unfinished. She scrolls for an hour, gets nothing done, and starts blaming herself — until she suddenly remembers: this week she already shipped 3 projects, answered dozens of emails, sat through back-to-back calls. Her brain doesn't want to run today. So be it.

![Moment 3](/characters/03-07-2026-chung-3.png)

It's evening now, and the project is still sitting there. But instead of forcing herself to open the laptop again, Linh steps out onto the balcony, watches the sky for a while, makes a cup of tea, and goes to bed early. Not giving up — just not that kind of day.

> It's okay if the brain rests for a day.
> Tomorrow it runs again, and that's fine.

Have you let yourself rest today?
