The 7th email says the same line: "We've decided to move forward with another candidate." The first time, it stung a little. By the 7th, Phuc just wants to close the laptop.

![Moment 1](/characters/05-07-2026-chung-1.png)

23, fresh out of college, he's sent his resume to 7 companies in 6 weeks. Two never replied, five said no — some through an automated email, some over a phone call. The same line, repeated enough times, stops hurting and just goes numb.

![Moment 2](/characters/05-07-2026-chung-2.png)

This morning he opens his resume file again and reads it from the top. Twice. Nothing wrong that he can find. Not because it's perfect — sometimes the problem was never really him.

![Moment 3](/characters/05-07-2026-chung-3.png)

He opens a job site, scrolls once — finds 3 listings that look like a fit. Not sending guarantees nothing. Sending at least leaves a chance. He opens his email and sends all 3.

> Seven times isn't the end.
> Maybe the 8th is the one.

Which attempt did you stop at?
