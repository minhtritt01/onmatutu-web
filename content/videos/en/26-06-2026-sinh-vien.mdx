10pm, her café shift over, the smell of coffee still on her shirt. She gets home, showers fast, eats fast. To her classmates she's just an ordinary student — none of them know she spent the last eight hours behind the counter.

![Moment 1](/characters/26-06-2026-sinh-vien-1.png)

Before she can even lie down, she opens her notebook. Her classmates think studying is all she does, so she lets them think it. The rest — the night shift, the rent, the late hours — she carries on her own, without telling anyone.

![Moment 2](/characters/26-06-2026-sinh-vien-2.png)

2am, her eyes stinging over macroeconomics, her phone lights up: one friend posting from a bar, another's story live from Đà Lạt. She looks for exactly one second, then turns off the screen. It's not that she doesn't want to go — it's that if she stops tonight, tomorrow she'll come up short on rent.

![Moment 3](/characters/26-06-2026-sinh-vien-3.png)

She posts nothing, tells no one. Not a single person knows how hard she's working to keep both the shift and her grades. But she knows. And for her, that's enough.

> No post, no one sees me trying.
> I see it, and that's enough.

Some effort only feels true when no one is watching. Are you quietly working at something you haven't told anyone about?
