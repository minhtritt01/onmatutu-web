Today Linh opened her phone and saw a message in the old class group chat: Minh just got a job at a foreign company. Below it, a string of heart emojis and "Congratulations!!!"

Linh liked the post. Closed her phone. Then sat staring at the spreadsheet open on her laptop — her current job, 9 million a month, nothing special to report.

It wasn't jealousy. It was something stranger — something that sat between being proud of your friend and quietly worried about yourself. *Everyone's moving. What am I doing?*

---

Linh got up to grab some water. Walking down the narrow office hallway, she passed a window — and outside, a small tree was blooming. White flowers, tiny, nameless. Last week it had just been a green tree. She'd walked past it every day without noticing.

But today she noticed.

Nobody photographed that tree. Nobody posted it. It just grew, just bloomed, at its own pace — without needing anyone to know, without being compared to the tree next door.

---

Linh went back to her desk. Opened the spreadsheet again.

Not because the feeling had disappeared. But because the work still needed doing. And maybe — not everyone blooms in the same season. Some bloom early, some bloom late. What matters is still growing, in your own way, even when no one is watching.
